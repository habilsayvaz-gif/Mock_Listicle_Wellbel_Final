01

The internal support your follicles depended on quietly changed

Most women know estrogen declines at menopause.

What almost nobody explains is what estrogen was doing for your hair specifically.

Research published in Menopause and Dermatology Research and Practice has linked estrogen to the regulation of the hair growth cycle — helping maintain a longer active growth phase and a more supportive follicle environment. It's not glamorous biology. But it's likely part of why your hair grew thick and reliably for most of your adult life without you having to think about it.

As estrogen declines, that support changes. The growth phase may shorten. More hairs can shift into the resting phase earlier. Over time, visible density can change — not because your follicles are gone, but because the internal conditions they depend on have shifted.

This isn't a cosmetic issue. It's a biological one. No shampoo or serum can reach it — because the shift is happening beneath the surface.

What this means

Supporting your hair after 45 may require addressing the internal environment — not just what you apply externally.

02

Your bloodwork came back normal. Your follicles may tell a different story.

This is the moment that makes most women feel invisible.

They bring the drain photos to the doctor. The doctor runs labs. Everything comes back in range. And they're sent home with "it's probably just stress."

Here's what standard bloodwork often doesn't capture.

As estrogen declines, follicles may become more vulnerable to androgen-related effects — including DHT sensitivity. DHT was always present in small amounts. But estrogen previously helped buffer its impact at the follicle level. As that buffer changes, those effects may become more relevant.

Over time, this can contribute to a process called follicular miniaturization — where strands gradually become finer and shorter with each growth cycle. The hair is still there. It's just that individual strands can become significantly less dense-looking over time.¹

This is why the ponytail looks similar in the mirror but feels thinner when you wrap it.

Standard labs measure hormone levels in the blood. They don't measure what's happening at the follicle level. And that gap — between what shows up on paper and what you experience every morning — is real.

"I had completely normal labs. My doctor kept telling me nothing was wrong. But I could see it happening every time I got in the shower." Verified Wellbel user

What this means

Normal bloodwork doesn't always reflect the full picture of what's happening in the midlife follicle environment.

03

Your scalp is a different growing environment than it was ten years ago

There's a reason scalp serums and thickening sprays often feel like temporary fixes.

They can improve how hair looks on the surface. But after 45, part of the issue may be the scalp environment itself — beneath the surface, where topical products can't reach.

During midlife, collagen production slows. The scalp tissue that houses your follicles can become thinner and less resilient. Research suggests that changes in scalp microcirculation with age can affect how efficiently oxygen and nutrients reach follicle roots — meaning the delivery infrastructure changes even when your diet doesn't.²

You're doing the right things. Eating well. Taking your supplements. But the road that used to carry those nutrients reliably to your follicle roots has narrowed. What used to arrive in full may now be arriving in less.

What this means

After 45, internal follicle support may matter more than any topical treatment applied externally.

04

The nutrients you're taking may not be reaching your follicles as efficiently as you think

This is the uncomfortable one. But it may explain more than anything else on this list.

Hair is considered a non-essential tissue. When the body is under stress — hormonal, physical, or nutritional — it tends to redirect resources toward essential systems first. Hair waits.

At the same time, research suggests digestive efficiency and nutrient absorption can shift with age. The body's ability to break down and use certain nutrients — including zinc, B vitamins, folate, and vitamin D — may become less optimal during midlife.³

This creates what might be called an absorption gap.

A woman takes her hair supplement faithfully for months. She sees almost nothing. She concludes supplements don't work. But the issue may not be the ingredients — it may be whether her body, at this stage of life, is processing and delivering them efficiently enough to reach the follicle.

"I took biotin every single day for almost a year. Switched to Wellbel and actually started noticing changes within a few months. I think my body just wasn't using the other stuff the way it needed to." Verified Wellbel reviewer

What this means

Getting the right nutrients into a capsule is only half the job. Supporting your body's ability to use them is the other half.

05

Your hair didn't change overnight. It just looked that way.

Most women describe the thinning as sudden. One month the ponytail was fine. A few months later it wasn't.

But here's what was actually building beneath the surface — often months before anything showed up in the mirror.

Hair grows in cycles. During midlife, that cycle can shorten — hairs may exit the growth phase earlier, and when they grow back, the strand that replaces them can come in finer than the one before it. This compounds quietly. Cycle after cycle. Often for six months to a year before the density change becomes visible.

This wasn't sudden. You didn't miss a window. The biology was moving slowly underneath long before you saw it.

The earlier you support the follicle environment, the better positioned your hair may be going into the next cycle. Each cycle is an opportunity.

The Science Behind It

What the research points to

Before the product — a quick look at what the science actually suggests.

  • 01

    Hormone changes during perimenopause and menopause have been linked in multiple studies to shifts in the hair growth cycle, follicle sensitivity, and scalp health.¹

  • 02

    Nutritional factors — particularly vitamin D, zinc, iron, folate, and B vitamins — are among the most commonly discussed in the context of female hair thinning during and after midlife.³

  • 03

    A 2022 study published in the journal Menopause found that more than half of postmenopausal women reported experiencing female pattern hair thinning — making this one of the most common and least addressed transitions women go through.⁴

  • 04

    Research into digestive efficiency and age suggests that how well the body absorbs and converts key nutrients can change over time — independent of diet quality.³

This is not a cosmetic edge case. It affects the majority of women who go through menopause. And it needs a solution built around that reality.

Wellbel Women+ is physician-formulated to help target the root causes behind compromised hair health leading up to and during menopause. Women over 45 who have not yet experienced perimenopause typically still benefit from the Women+ formulation.

I'm younger than 45 and already in perimenopause. Should I take Women+?

Yes. Wellbel Women+ was developed for those needing additional support as they experience hormone-related hair changes. If you're younger than 45 and experiencing perimenopause, Women+ can help support the integrity of your hair as you go through these changes.

How long before I notice a difference?

Most women see meaningful changes in 3–6 months, in line with the natural hair growth cycle. Some women report less shedding within the first month — visible density typically follows in months 3–6.

Will it cause hair to grow in places other than my head?

Wellbel Women+ was formulated to support healthy hair where it thins. Members of our community have reported additional growth and thickness in their eyebrows and eyelashes — but not unwanted hair in other places.

What's the recommended usage?

Three capsules per day, taken at any time — we recommend with food for optimal absorption. You can also open the capsules and stir them into a smoothie or yogurt.

Can I take it alongside other supplements I'm already on?

Generally, yes. Wellbel Women+ is designed as a complete daily hair-support formula, so most women find they can stop their separate biotin / B-complex / vitamin D once they start. If you're on any prescription medication or have a medical condition, check with your physician before adding any supplement.
